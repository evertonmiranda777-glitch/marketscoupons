Part 1 — The Variable

The single most consequential rule in any prop firm evaluation is the drawdown rule. Three types exist:

  1. Trailing intraday drawdown — DD line follows the highest unrealized equity reached at any moment in any session.
  2. Trailing EOD drawdown — DD line follows the highest end-of-day equity.
  3. Static drawdown — DD line fixed at account start minus DD amount. Never moves.

These three sound similar. They are not. The mathematical implications are radically different. A trader who would survive trailing EOD might blow up under trailing intraday with the exact same strategy.

Part 2 — Trailing Intraday

The DD line follows the highest unrealized equity reached during any session.

How it works

You start with a $50,000 account. Trailing DD = $2,500. DD line at $47,500.
You enter a long trade at $50,000. Trade runs unrealized to +$1,500. DD line moves to $49,000.
Trade reverses. You close at +$500 (unrealized peak was $1,500).
Your closing equity is $50,500. But DD line is still at $49,000 (the high-water mark from the unrealized $51,500 peak).
Your effective remaining buffer: $50,500 - $49,000 = $1,500.

Why this is brutal

Wicks lock the DD line higher permanently. A morning of volatility that wicks high then reverts to flat leaves you with a permanently tighter DD buffer. Over a session of 10 trades, the trailing DD can compress your effective risk budget by 30-50% even if your end-of-day P&L is zero.

Part 3 — Trailing EOD

The DD line follows only the end-of-day equity (or some firms: highest realized equity).

How it works

Same $50,000 account. Trailing EOD DD = $2,500. DD line at $47,500.
You enter long at $50,000. Trade runs unrealized to +$1,500. DD line does NOT move (intraday wick ignored).
Trade reverses. You close at +$500. Now the DD line evaluates: end-of-day equity = $50,500.
DD line moves to $48,000.
Your effective remaining buffer: $50,500 - $48,000 = $2,500. Identical to start.

Why this is more forgiving

Intraday volatility doesn't compress your buffer. Only closing equity matters. A swing trader who holds positions overnight without realizing profits keeps the full DD buffer regardless of intraday wicks.

Part 4 — Static

The DD line is fixed at account-start minus DD amount. Never moves.

How it works

$100,000 account. Static DD = $10,000. DD line at $90,000.
Forever. Until you blow up or scale the account.

Why this is the easiest

No surprises. The DD line is fixed. You always know exactly how much risk you can absorb. Best for swing traders and conservative styles.

Why it's rare

Firms offering static DD typically charge higher entry fees, require longer evals, or impose other constraints. The math favors trader; the cost reflects that.

Part 5 — Worked Examples

Let's trace the same trading sequence across all three DD models.

Scenario

$50,000 account. $2,500 DD limit. You trade 5 sessions. Each session: enter long, position runs unrealized to +$1,000, then reverts. You close at break-even.

Trailing intraday outcome

Each session, DD line moves up $500 (proportionate to wick from $0 unrealized to +$500 above last DD water mark).
After 5 sessions: DD line at $47,500 + (5 × $500) = $50,000.
Your equity: $50,000.
Effective remaining buffer: $0. You are at DD limit despite zero net P&L.

Trailing EOD outcome

Each session, intraday wicks ignored. End-of-day equity stays at $50,000.
DD line stays at $47,500.
After 5 sessions: Effective remaining buffer: $2,500. Unchanged.

Static outcome

DD line never moves. Stays at $47,500.
After 5 sessions: Effective remaining buffer: $2,500. Unchanged.

The same trader, same strategy, same outcomes — but under trailing intraday the trader is at the DD wall with zero PnL, while under EOD or static the trader has full buffer.

Part 6 — Why Trailing Intraday Destroys Most Funded Accounts

Statistics from prop firm transparency reports:

  • Apex funded account median survival: ~75 days.
  • Bulenox funded account median survival: ~120 days.
  • FTMO Swing funded account median survival: ~180 days.

The DD type predicts survival. Why?

  1. Wicks compress buffer continuously.
  2. Wicks are invisible to traders watching only closed P&L.
  3. Emotional trading produces wicks (entering large, reverting before close).
  4. A bad week of revenge trading can wick the DD into termination range even with break-even net P&L.

The conclusion: trailing intraday is structurally hostile to traders who hold positions for more than 30 minutes. Apex made the choice to use trailing intraday deliberately — they want disciplined fast scalpers, not swing traders.

Part 10 — The Mental Game of Trailing DD

Trailing intraday DD creates a constant background anxiety. Every unrealized profit move is simultaneously good (PnL) and bad (DD line moving up). Many traders cannot tolerate this dissonance long-term.

Symptoms of trailing-DD-driven burnout:

  • Closing winning trades far too early to "lock in" before DD line moves.
  • Refusing to enter new trades after profitable mornings.
  • Compulsive equity-checking through the day.
  • Reduced position size to avoid wicks.

If your style is not naturally scalp-oriented, trailing intraday will produce these symptoms within 1-3 months. The right answer is to switch to a trailing EOD or static firm — not to "tough it out."

Part 11 — Top 5 Mistakes That Trigger Trailing DD

  1. Not understanding which DD type the firm uses. Most common mistake. Solve by reading the firm's rules carefully before signing up.
  2. Treating trailing intraday like static. Underestimating how aggressively wicks compress the buffer.
  3. Adding to winning positions past safe profit lock. Larger size means larger wicks. Especially destructive pre-profit-lock.
  4. Holding positions overnight on trailing intraday. Overnight gaps can pin DD outside your control.
  5. Not journaling DD percentage daily. The DD line is invisible if you don't track it actively.

Frequently Asked Questions

Difference between trailing and EOD drawdown?

Trailing intraday: DD line follows highest unrealized equity. Trailing EOD: DD line follows end-of-day equity only.

Which firms use which?

Trailing intraday: Apex. Trailing EOD: Bulenox, TakeProfit. Static: FTMO Swing, FundedNext.

Is trailing intraday a scam?

No. Designed to favor scalpers. Hostile to swing traders. Match style to firm.

What is profit lock?

Threshold at which trailing DD converts to static. Removes the trailing pressure once you've earned a buffer.

Can I switch firms once funded?

Can't transfer. But can hold multiple funded accounts across firms.

How does drawdown affect scaling?

Scaling typically resets DD proportionally. Check specific firm rules.

Is static always better?

Mathematically yes. Often comes with higher cost or longer eval. Trade-offs exist.

Most generous DD?

FTMO Swing static. For trailing: Bulenox EOD most forgiving in practice.

How to track DD real-time?

Firm dashboard. Confirm daily. Track manually if unsure.

Negotiate DD rules?

No. Choose a firm whose terms match.

Why do firms use trailing DD?

Reduces firm risk. Aligns trader behavior with firm survival.

Median funded account lifespan?

~90 days overall. Trailing intraday: ~75 days. EOD: ~120 days. Static: ~180 days.

Conclusion

The drawdown variable decides your funded account survival as much as your trading skill does. Trailing intraday is unforgiving to anyone whose style produces wicks. Trailing EOD is forgiving but still constraints. Static is the easiest but rarely available.

Match the math to your style. A swing trader on Apex (trailing intraday) is fighting the structure of the account in addition to fighting the market. A scalper on FTMO Swing (static) is leaving easy structural advantage on the table by paying for capability they don't need.
