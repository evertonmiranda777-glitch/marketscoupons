The implication is that the SPX intraday tape you watch in 2026 has different dynamics than the SPX intraday tape you watched in 2021. Specifically:

  • Dealer hedge book turnover went from days/weeks to hours.
  • The dominant Greek shifted from gamma (price sensitivity) to charm (time decay of delta).

The trader who doesn't understand 0DTE is operating in 2026 with a 2021 mental model.

Part 2 — Timeline

CBOE introduced daily expiration in stages:

  • 2005 — Weekly options launched on SPX.
  • 2016 — Wednesday expirations added.
  • May 2022 — Tuesday and Thursday expirations added. Now four expirations per week.
  • November 2022 — Monday and Friday expirations added. Daily expirations now complete for SPX.

The completion of daily expiries in November 2022 was the regulatory catalyst. The volume migration followed within months.

Part 3 — Why 0DTE Exists

Four real use cases drove the growth:

1. Institutional tail risk hedging

Hedge funds with overnight directional exposure want to insure intraday tail moves without paying for overnight optionality. 0DTE provides exactly that — buy puts at 9:30 AM, let them expire worthless or harvest gains by 4 PM. No overnight cost.

2. Income strategies

Premium-selling funds — Cambria, Simplify, and others — generate income by systematically selling 0DTE premium with hedge structures. Theta decay over a single trading day is rapid and predictable; structured premium selling captures it.

3. Retail speculation

Leveraged directional bets with defined max loss. A $5 premium on a near-the-money SPX call can deliver 10× returns on a 1% intraday move. The lottery dynamic attracts retail.

4. Algorithmic gamma scalping

Algorithmic desks running long-gamma strategies use 0DTE to scalp small intraday moves while collecting theta. The strategy is mechanical and works at scale.

Part 4 — How 0DTE Rebuilt the Dealer Book

Before 2022, the SPX dealer hedge book aged over days and weeks. A dealer who sold a 30-day put on a Monday managed that book over the following 22 trading days. Gamma — the sensitivity of delta to price — dominated risk management because the book persisted long enough for price-driven Greek shifts to matter most.

After 2022, the book turns over every single trading day. A dealer's overnight book is largely accumulated during the day and unwound by the close. Charm — the time decay of delta — now dominates because the time variable changes faster than the price variable for the typical 0DTE position.

The implication is structural and permanent. The 0DTE dealer book mechanically forces hedging at specific times of day (mostly the last 90 minutes), creating new intraday patterns that did not exist before.

Part 5 — Charm vs Gamma

Gamma

The rate of change of delta with respect to price. High gamma = small price moves cause large delta shifts. Dealers hedge gamma by trading shares/futures to neutralize their effective exposure.

Charm

The rate of change of delta with respect to time. Even with no price move, delta decays toward 0 (for OTM) or +/-1 (for ITM) as expiration approaches.

In 0DTE

Time-to-expiry is hours, not weeks. Charm accelerates exponentially as expiration approaches. By 2 PM ET, charm dominates gamma for ATM contracts. By 3 PM ET, the entire dealer hedge ratio is dominated by charm-driven adjustments. Dealers must rebalance continuously.

The result

The mechanical, time-driven rebalancing creates a persistent intraday pattern that did not exist before 2022. Specifically: the last 90 minutes show dramatically different dynamics than pre-2022 sessions.

Part 6 — The 3 PM Ramp

The single most-watched intraday phenomenon in modern markets. The 3 PM ramp is the mechanical price drift that occurs in the last 90 minutes of an SPX session, driven by dealer charm rebalancing.

Mechanism

As charm accelerates, dealers must rebalance their delta hedge. If they are net short gamma (common in 0DTE-heavy days), they rebalance by buying as price rises and selling as price falls — but in 0DTE this rebalancing happens with shrinking time windows, producing a quasi-trending drift toward the largest gamma wall.

Statistics

Average final-hour SPX move pre-2022: ±0.12%. Average post-2022: ±0.42%. The 3.5× increase in late-day volatility is almost entirely attributable to 0DTE charm dynamics.

Trading implications

  • Mean-reversion trades in the 9:30-2:30 window have similar priors to pre-2022.
  • Mean-reversion trades in the 2:30-4:00 window have dramatically reduced priors — the ramp dominates.
  • Trend-following in the final 90 minutes has elevated priors when GEX is negative.
  • Late-day trades should anticipate, not chase, the gamma wall pinning.

Part 7 — The Volatility Crush Trade

Premium decays through the day. By 4 PM, ATM 0DTE premium is approximately zero (the option is at the strike). The decay is steep and predictable.

The structural trade

  1. Sell ATM straddle at 9:30 AM (collect premium).
  2. Manage delta exposure by rebalancing with futures hedges.
  3. Close position at 3:30 PM or earlier if profit target hit.

Expected value

Over hundreds of sessions, the structural trade has positive expectancy — typically 0.5-1.5R per trade on average. The variance is high; single sessions can produce -3R losses. The strategy survives only with proper sizing and consistent execution.

Why retail fails at this

  • Improper sizing — over-leveraged on a single trade.
  • No hedge structure — naked premium selling on a tail-risk session.
  • Emotional closing — exiting losing trades early without disciplined exits.
  • Ignoring catalyst days — selling premium into FOMC or CPI releases.

Part 8 — 0DTE Iron Condors

The most popular structured 0DTE retail strategy. Sell an OTM call, sell an OTM put, buy further-OTM versions of each as protection. Max profit = the credit received. Max loss = the width minus credit.

Strike selection

Typical 0DTE iron condor: sell strikes at 1 standard deviation OTM (approximately 16-delta). Buy protection 5-10 points further OTM.

Math

SPX at 5,500. Sell 5,550 call ($3.00 credit), sell 5,450 put ($3.00 credit). Buy 5,560 call ($1.20 debit), buy 5,440 put ($1.20 debit). Net credit: $3.60. Max loss: $10 width - $3.60 credit = $6.40. Risk/reward: $6.40 risk for $3.60 max profit. Break-even win rate needed: 64%.

Historical win rate on 1-SD iron condors: ~65-72%. The strategy has slight positive expectancy on average but requires disciplined execution.

Part 9 — The Gamma Wall

Strikes with the most positive net gamma act as magnets on expiration day. Price gravitates toward them in the last hour because dealer hedging mechanically pulls in that direction.

Identifying the wall

Read total open interest by strike on SPX 0DTE pre-open. The strike with the highest sum of call OI plus put OI is typically the gamma wall. Tools like SqueezeMetrics, SpotGamma, and Volland publish daily gamma wall data.

Trading the wall

  • If price is above the wall by 1 PM, lean short into the close.
  • If price is below the wall by 1 PM, lean long into the close.
  • If price is within 0.3% of the wall, expect pinning behavior.

The gamma wall trade has ~60% win rate when conditions are clean (no major catalyst, normal volatility regime). Win rate collapses on high-VIX or news-driven sessions.

Part 10 — 0DTE and Index Futures

0DTE dealer hedging executes in ES and NQ futures. This means SPX futures moves in 2026 are increasingly downstream of options dealer flow rather than equity flow.

The spillover

  • ES tracks SPX more tightly than ever — because both are governed by the same dealer hedge logic.
  • ES intraday volatility patterns now show the same 3 PM ramp signature as SPX.
  • Futures traders ignoring 0DTE dealer positioning are trading partial information.

The practical implication: futures traders should monitor SPX gamma exposure data, even if they never trade options. The gamma profile predicts intraday futures behavior.

Part 11 — Why 95% of 0DTE Buyers Lose

Academic research (Lo et al. 2023, Jiang et al. 2024) consistently finds that retail 0DTE BUYERS lose money on aggregate. The numbers:

  • Retail 0DTE call buyers: ~92% lose over a 6-month sample.
  • Retail 0DTE put buyers: ~94% lose.
  • Retail straddle buyers: ~88% lose.

Why

Three structural reasons:

  1. Theta decay: premium melts rapidly through the day. The buyer fights an exponentially-decaying clock.
  2. Dealer skew: dealers price 0DTE premium above fair value (the implied volatility skew at very short expiries). The buyer pays a premium for the right to gamble.
  3. Transaction costs: spreads + commissions consume a meaningful fraction of expected returns.

The asymmetry retail does not see

Professional traders sell 0DTE much more than they buy. The structural edge sits on the sell side. Retail flow concentrates on the buy side. The net result is a continuous wealth transfer from retail buyers to professional sellers.

Part 13 — Case Studies

Case 1 — August 5, 2024 — Yen Carry Unwind

The yen carry unwind tested 0DTE dealer capacity in real time. SPX gapped down 3% on the open. Dealer hedge books were structurally short gamma entering the session. The mechanical hedging cascade:

  1. 9:30 AM: gap down 3%. Dealers sell into the gap to rebalance.
  2. 10:30 AM: VIX hits 65 intraday — highest since 2020.
  3. 11:00 AM: charm pressure builds. Dealers continue selling.
  4. 1:00 PM: aggressive bottom formation. Long-gamma funds step in.
  5. 3:00 PM: ramp reverses. Closing SPX -3.0% from prior close (off the -6% lows).

The 0DTE dealer cascade amplified the move and then mechanically pulled price back. A trader who recognized the dynamic and faded the morning panic was well-positioned for the afternoon recovery.

Case 2 — Volmageddon February 5, 2018 — 0DTE Echo

The original short-gamma dealer cascade. While not technically 0DTE (those didn't exist yet), the Volmageddon mechanic is identical to what 0DTE risks could produce. VIX exploded from 13 to 50 in hours as dealer hedging mechanically amplified a 4% SPX decline.

The lesson for 0DTE: when dealer books are net short gamma, fundamental news catalysts produce 2-3× larger moves than the news warrants. This is why VIX spikes faster in 2026 than in 2018.

Case 3 — COVID March 2020 — What 0DTE Did and Did Not Do

COVID predated daily 0DTE expiries. SPX dropped 34% in 23 days. The mechanism was dealer hedging on weekly and monthly expiries — slower than today's 0DTE dynamics but similar in structure. The crisis showed that mechanical hedging can amplify moves significantly when sentiment shifts violently.

If COVID happened in a 0DTE-dominant market, the move would likely have been faster (3-5 days instead of 23) and possibly larger.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is 0DTE?

Zero days to expiration options — contracts that expire on the same trading day. Available daily on SPX since November 2022.

Are 0DTE options gambling?

Buying 0DTE for directional speculation has lottery-like odds: 95%+ of buyers lose. Selling 0DTE premium with proper hedging has positive expectancy. The instrument is neutral; the strategy determines the outcome.

Can I trade 0DTE with $5K?

Technically yes, with SPY 0DTE or micro-contracts. Practically, sizing constraints make meaningful PnL difficult. A funded prop account of $50K+ allows professional-grade 0DTE strategies.

What is the assignment risk on 0DTE?

SPX 0DTE is cash-settled and European-style — no early assignment risk. SPY 0DTE is American-style and can be assigned ITM. Stick to SPX for clean cash settlement.

Why are 0DTE so popular?

Three reasons: institutional tail hedging without overnight cost, income strategies harvesting steep theta decay, retail speculation with defined max loss.

Difference between 0DTE on SPX vs SPY?

SPX: cash-settled European, 1256 tax treatment, 10x notional. SPY: American with assignment risk, standard tax treatment.

Can I scalp 0DTE intraday?

Yes, but bid-ask spreads compound. Focus on a few high-prior moments per day.

Typical bid-ask spread?

ATM SPX 0DTE: 5-15 cents. Far OTM: 5-20 cents (large relative to premium).

Are 0DTE worth it without options skill?

No. Greeks all interact in 0DTE. Without baseline options literacy, guaranteed losses.

How does 0DTE affect futures traders?

Dealer hedging executes in ES/NQ. Futures intraday is increasingly downstream of options dealer flow. Monitor SPX gamma exposure even if you only trade futures.

Margin call risk?

Defined-risk strategies have capped loss. Naked short 0DTE requires portfolio margin and carries unlimited risk.
