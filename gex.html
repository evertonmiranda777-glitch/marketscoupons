What is Gamma Exposure (GEX)?

What is GEX?

Gamma Exposure (GEX) measures the options volume that market makers (large banks and institutions) need to hedge at each price level. When a market maker sells an option, they must buy or sell the underlying asset to protect themselves. GEX shows WHERE and HOW MUCH they need to do this.

Zero Gamma (Gamma Flip)

The Zero Gamma level is where total exposure flips from positive to negative. Above zero gamma, market makers buy dips and sell rallies (suppress volatility). Below zero gamma, they do the opposite and AMPLIFY movements. It's the most important level of the day.

Put Wall & Call Wall

The Put Wall is the strike with the largest put options concentration — it acts as strong support because market makers buy the asset at this level. The Call Wall is the opposite — largest call concentration, acts as resistance because they sell there.

HVL (High Volume Level)

The HVL is the strike with the highest absolute gamma volume. Price tends to "stick" to this level during the day because market makers are constantly hedging there. If price breaks through HVL with force, it usually signals a strong directional move.

Positive vs Negative GEX

Positive GEX (total > 0): Market is "pinned" — market makers absorb moves. Tight range days. Negative GEX (total < 0): Market is "loose" — moves are amplified. High volatility days. This is when stops get swept and trends accelerate.

How to use in trading?

Use Zero Gamma as the key level of the day. If price is above it, trade mean reversions. If below, trade breakouts and trends. The Put Wall is reliable support and the Call Wall is resistance. Combine with your technical analysis to increase accuracy.
