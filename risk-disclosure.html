3. Risks of Trading Futures and Forex

Trading futures and forex involves substantial risk and is not appropriate for all investors. Specific risks include, but are not limited to:

  • Leverage risk, small adverse price movements may result in losses exceeding your initial deposit.
  • Volatility risk, markets may move rapidly due to news, economic data releases, or geopolitical events.
  • Liquidity risk, under certain conditions, it may be difficult or impossible to liquidate a position at a desired price.
  • System and connectivity risk, execution may be delayed or interrupted by failures in your internet connection, broker platform, or third-party services.
  • Counterparty risk, broker, exchange, or prop firm insolvency may impact your ability to recover funds or simulated balances.
  • Regulatory risk, laws and rules governing trading may change without notice and affect your ability to operate or withdraw funds.
