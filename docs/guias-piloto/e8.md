## <a id="drawdown"></a>The 3% / 8% Drawdown Math (Why It Catches New Traders)

The single rule most new E8 traders underestimate is the **3% daily drawdown on Signature**. Here's what that actually means on a $100K account:

- **Starting balance:** $100,000
- **Daily drawdown threshold:** $3,000 below end-of-prior-day equity (or starting equity, whichever is higher, static, not trailing)
- **Total drawdown threshold:** $8,000 below starting balance (static)

### The trap

Signature's **3% daily** is **tighter than the 4–5% most peer firms run**. If you enter a trade after a good day and your account is up $4,000, the **daily drawdown resets to the $104,000 equity high of end-of-day**, but **only at market close**. Intraday, you still have $3,000 of daily buffer. One volatile session where you stop out twice can end your challenge even though you're *net profitable* on the week.

### How to respect it

1. **Size trades to risk ≤1% per position** ($1,000 on $100K). Three losers in a row = $3,000 = you're out. Two losers + a scratch = you still have room.
2. **Never add to a loser**. On Signature, averaging down is what kills the daily DD faster than anything else.
3. **Stop trading after 2 losses in a single session**. Protect the day. There's no prize for making the 6% target in three sessions vs thirty.
4. **Watch the 8% total DD as the real ceiling**, the daily is a speed bump; the 8% total is the hard stop. A $92,000 equity = game over no matter what.

E8 One's looser **5% daily / 10% total** is noticeably more forgiving. If you're a swing trader or new to the 3% daily psychology, **One is the safer product**.

## <a id="scaling"></a>Scaling to $1M

E8 publishes a scaling plan that claims up to **$1M total allocation**, with **$400K in simulated capital** as the primary funded tier. Here's how the stair-step works:

### Signature scaling

Trade profitably on your funded account. When you:
- Reach **10% total profit over any 6-month rolling window**
- Maintain **no breaches** during that window
- Trade a **minimum of 30 days** during the window

You're eligible for a **25% account-size increase**. So $100K → $125K → $156K → ... up to the $400K simulated cap. Beyond that, E8's higher-tier program offers additional allocation toward the $1M claimed ceiling, subject to sustained performance.

### One scaling

Same mechanics, 10% over 6 months, 25% uplift, applied to E8 One accounts.

### Reality check

Scaling programs in this industry are marketing ceilings, not typical outcomes. Most funded traders **never reach the first scaling tier** because they breach before 6 months of disciplined trading. That's not a knock on E8, it's true at every firm. Treat the $1M as "possible in theory, rare in practice" and plan around your first $100K as the real product.

## <a id="how-to-pass"></a>How to Pass an E8 Challenge (Step-by-Step)

1. **Pick your track.** Forex = MT5. Futures = Match-Trader. Crypto = MT5/cTrader. Don't buy a track you don't already trade, the eval is not the place to learn a new market.
2. **Pick Signature or One.** Signature if you can hit 6% in one shot without tripping 3% daily. One if you want the 5%/10% buffer.
3. **Start small.** Buy the $25K or $50K tier first. Validate the platform and your edge before scaling your challenge spend.
4. **Respect the 3-day minimum.** On Signature, you need 3 trading days minimum before the target counts. Don't scalp through the target in 6 hours, E8 checks.
5. **Risk ≤1% per trade.** On $100K Signature, max $1,000 risk per position. Three losses = your daily DD budget gone.
6. **Stop after 2 losses in a session.** Every session. No exceptions. This single rule saves more challenges than any strategy does.
7. **Hit the target.** 6% for Signature, 8% + 5% for One. Journal every trade.
8. **Move to funded.** 3 days of funded trading, then request your first payout.
9. **Accumulate 10% over 6 months for scaling.** Slow and clean beats fast and reckless.
10. **Apply for Bali Summit** if you hit the funded-trader selection criteria. Worth the trip.

## <a id="simulated"></a>Regulatory Framing: Simulated Capital Explained

E8, like most retail prop firms in 2026, operates on a **simulated-capital model**. Here's what that actually means, in plain English:

- **Your funded account is not a live brokerage account.** It's a demo environment that mirrors real market data.
- **Your P&L is calculated from simulated fills** at real market prices.
- **Payouts are funded by E8's balance sheet**, not by "your share of broker profits."
- **Payouts are technically discretionary** under the terms, E8 reserves the right to terminate accounts for rule breaches, suspicious activity (hedging across accounts, latency arbitrage, HFT exploits), or credible fraud indicators.

### What this means for you

- **The $71M paid out is real.** It came out of E8's revenue and has been paid to traders.
- **But your individual payout is discretionary in the contract.** If E8 detects a rule violation you didn't see, they can cancel a payout.
- **Legitimate traders get paid.** The history and Trustpilot volume support this.
- **Don't mistake "simulated" for "fake."** The prop-firm industry restructured post-2023–2024 regulatory pressure to operate under clear educational/simulation framing. E8 was among the first to adopt this transparently.

If **fully-regulated broker capital** is a requirement for you, E8 (and virtually every retail prop firm) doesn't meet that bar. If you can operate within a simulated-capital contract that has paid out $71M, E8 is fine.

**$400K in simulated funded capital is the standard scaling ceiling.** The $1M claim refers to higher-tier allocation programs for top-performing traders. Achievable but rare, most traders don't scale past the first $100K → $125K jump.

An **annual invite-only in-person event** in Bali for selected funded traders. 3–4 days of workshops, mentorship, and community. It's a genuine perk that no other major prop firm offers at this level.
