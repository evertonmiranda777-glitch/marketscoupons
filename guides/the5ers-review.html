High-Stakes Challenge — The 2-Step Evaluation

The High-Stakes Challenge follows the classic 2-phase model popularized by FTMO and FundedNext. Pay the fee, hit Phase 1 target, hit Phase 2 target, get funded.

Rules (Typical)

  • Phase 1 profit target: 8%
  • Phase 2 profit target: 5%
  • Stop-out level: ~6%
  • Daily loss limit: ~3%
  • Time limit: Unlimited (no minimum calendar days on phases)
  • Profit split: 80–100% depending on performance tier

When to pick High-Stakes over Hyper Growth

  • You prefer a multi-phase framework with smaller per-phase targets
  • You want lower psychological pressure than Hyper Growth's single 10% sprint
  • You trade swing positions and need time to compound profits

The High-Stakes Challenge costs more than Hyper Growth on an apples-to-apples account size basis, but it's more forgiving. If you've failed a Hyper Growth run or want a second chance with softer mechanics, move here.

Bootcamp — The Beginner-Friendly Path

Bootcamp is The5ers' education-first program. Three phases with smaller targets, strict risk management rules, and access to the full Academy (classes, webinars, live masterminds). The goal isn't to get funded as fast as possible — it's to learn how to trade professionally over 2–6 months.

Typical Rules

  • 3 phases, each with ~6% profit target
  • Tighter daily loss limit (under 3% typically)
  • Minimum profitable days requirement per phase
  • Journaling and risk-management modules required before certain milestones
  • Full Academy access (classes, webinars, weekly masterminds)

Who Bootcamp is for

  • New forex traders (under 1 year experience)
  • Traders who've failed multiple challenges at other firms
  • Traders who value structured education as much as funding
  • Traders with a small monthly budget who want a long runway

Bootcamp is the slowest path to a funded account, but it's also the one that produces the highest long-term success rate for traders who stick with it. The Academy alone is worth the subscription fee — $500+ worth of trading education included.

Drawdown and Risk Rules

The5ers uses static balance-based drawdown across all programs. Unlike trailing models (Apex, TPT intraday), your floor doesn't move up with profits — it's set once on the starting balance and stays there.

Hyper Growth Specifics

  • Stop-out level: 6% of initial balance (hard floor, account closes)
  • Daily loss limit: 3% of initial balance (resets at broker's daily close)
  • Floating losses count — open P&L is measured, not just closed

Progression Rules

Once you pass, the rules adjust slightly for the funded account:

  • Same percentages (6% stop-out, 3% daily)
  • But measured on the funded account balance, which scales as you grow
  • After scaling milestones, drawdown adjusts proportionally

What kills accounts most often

  1. Hit daily loss — 3% on a $5K account = only $150 daily loss allowance. Tight.
  2. Hit stop-out — 6% = $300 total drawdown on the $5K. Miss a single 6% run and you're out.
  3. Leverage misuse — 1:30 is moderate; using the full 1:30 on a major pair with a tight stop can blow through 3% in one candle if spread widens on news.

Critical tip: position-size for ~1% risk per trade max on Hyper Growth. That gives you 3 losses before daily limit, 6 before stop-out.

Scaling to $4M — How It Works

The scaling program is The5ers' unique selling point. Most firms cap at $200K–$400K. The5ers takes qualified traders to $4 million through a clear milestone structure.

Scaling Mechanics

  1. Start: Funded account at your evaluated size ($5K–$100K typical).
  2. Hit profit milestones: Generate at least 10% cumulative profit AND complete a minimum number of profitable trading days.
  3. Request scaling: Account size increases by a fixed percentage (typically 25–100% per milestone depending on program tier).
  4. Repeat: Every cycle of ~3–6 months with consistent profitability unlocks the next scaling tier.
  5. Maximum: $4,000,000 total after ~10–15 successful scaling rounds (multi-year path for most traders).

Why $4M is realistic

The5ers is a private equity fund, not an evaluator. Your "funded account" at higher tiers is actual capital they're allocating to your strategy — not simulated execution. This is why they can scale to $4M: the math is capital allocation, not account issuance.

Most traders will realistically reach $50K–$500K over 1–3 years. $4M is the theoretical ceiling for top-1% performers.
