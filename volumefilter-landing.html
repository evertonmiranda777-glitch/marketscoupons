// How it works

3 colors. 1 glance. Direct decision.

VolumeFilter compares each candle against the 20-period volume average and paints the bar one solid color, you don't calculate anything.

GREEN

volume ≥ 1.5x average

Real aggression. Institutional buyer (or seller) actually pushing price. This is the candle that confirms entry.

GRAY

volume within normal range

Market breathing. Confirms nothing, could be absorption, could be indecision. Signal to wait for the next.

RED

volume below average

No fuel behind the move. End of a trend, it's exhaustion. On a breakout, it's fake, won't go far.

Default parameters: Period 20 · HighThreshold 1.5x. Adjustable in the indicator settings.
// The 5 trading patterns

VolumeFilter isn't just color. It's decision.

The 5 patterns that show up on the chart every day and what to do in each. No fluff, no theory. Straight to the point.

01
BREAKOUT

Breakout confirmation

Every level breakout (resistance, support, IBH, IBL, PDH, PDL, range) needs at least one green bar on the break candle. Without that, it's false until proven otherwise. A candle that breaks gray or red = stops being hit, not institutional conviction.

When to useOn every important level breakout. Don't trade the break without green confirmation.
CautionDon't enter with a gray bar hoping "the next one will confirm". The signal needs volume ON the break candle.
02
EXHAUSTION

Trend exhaustion

Every trend has a cycle: start (greens in a row), middle (gray mixed), end (predominantly red). The exhaustion signal is price continuing in the direction but volume turning red consistently, the classic price-volume divergence.

When to useIn a winning trade, deciding whether to take profit or hold. Useful in rallies without pullback.
Caution1–2 isolated reds is not exhaustion. Requires consistent pattern: 3+ in a row or 4 of the last 6.
03
CLIMAX

Climax volume

A green bar absurdly above the average (3x, 4x+) after a long directional sequence often marks the turning point, not the start. Counter-intuitive: instinct says "there's strength, it'll go far", but it's the last buyer entering (up) or last seller capitulating (down).

When to useIn stretched moves (5+ directional candles) and PDH/PDL tests. Take partial profit before reversal.
CautionClimax is not a counter-trend entry by itself. The reverse trade needs more confirmation.
04
ABSORPTION

Institutional absorption

The most subtle and valuable pattern: green bar on VolumeFilter but small candle in range (doji, pin bar, short body). Lots of volume traded, price standing still = someone large absorbing liquidity from the other side. Sign of strong reversal in the next candles.

When to useOn important level tests when price gets stuck with long wicks and small bodies. R/R is usually 3:1 or better.
CautionDon't confuse with low liquidity (gray/red + small candle = market sleeping). Absorption requires a GREEN bar.
05
DISCIPLINE

Dead day

The hardest pattern: requires doing nothing. Last 30–60 bars overwhelmingly gray/red + greens that don't sustain = dead day. On that day, any trade gets stopped by noise. The trade you didn't take is a winning trade.

When to useEvery morning before trading. Last 30 bars predominantly gray+red = low expectancy.
CautionDon't force a trade to "make up for" yesterday. That thinking burns beginner accounts.
